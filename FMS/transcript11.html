Group Discussion (GD)

Topic: "Simplicity is the sole reason for success" (paraphrased)

GD was a crucial part of the selection process as the same panelists evaluated both GD and PI. There were 8 participants with a total of 9 minutes to discuss.

I began the GD, spoke for more than a minute, provided contrasts with examples, and contributed 3-4 more times. I argued that while simplicity is important, it cannot be the sole reason for success. I also cited the example of Mr. Manohar Parrikar. Overall, I felt I performed well.

Personal Interview (PI)

Panelists: Two female professors (F1 & F2).

Me: Good morning, ma’am.

F1: Good morning.

Me: May I? (Pointing to the chair)

F1: Yes, please.

F1: So, X, you’re still studying, is it?

Me: Yes, ma’am.

F1: You’re studying in an NIT?

Me: Yes, ma’am. I’m in my final year pursuing Mechanical Engineering from NITK Surathkal.

F1: What have you done in college?

Me: *Told.*

F1: Tell us about the social initiative you’ve mentioned in your form.

Me: *Told.*

Extempore

F1: Okay, we’ll now give you a topic. You have to speak on it for 1 minute. Your topic is **CRY**.

I assumed it referred to emotional crying and began talking about how people cry in happiness and sadness. I mentioned a mother’s happiness when her baby cries for the first time.

F1 (interrupting): Do you even know what your topic is? It’s the foundation **CRY**.

Me: *Oh… (Realized my mistake).*

Both panelists chuckled.

Me: Should I restart?

F1: Yes, start again.

I had no idea about the CRY foundation but spoke a few generic lines.

F1 (interrupting again): Do you know what CRY does?

Me (sheepishly): No, ma'am.

F1: No problem. Know of any other organization?

Me: *Didn’t know much, so I said PETA.*

F1: Okay, so **PETA** is your topic.

Me: *Spoke about PETA for 40-45 seconds but struggled for content.*

F1: You still have time.

Me: *Said something irrelevant.*

F1: Okay, stop. Do you follow news?

Me: Yes, ma’am.

F1: Tell me some recent news.

Me: *Mentioned elections.*

F1: No, not elections. Something else.

Me: *Jet Airways crisis.*

F1: Anything else?

Me: *Tata Motors share price drop.*

F1: Why did the price fall?

Me: *Explained well.*

F1: What did they do to overcome this?

Me: *Didn’t know but still attempted an answer.*

F1: Who taught you all these fancy terms?

Me (smiling): Ma’am, the main aim of any business is profit maximization. (Blunder!)

F1: No, no, no. It's not to make profits.

Me: *Tried to correct—"Sorry ma’am, it’s to maximize wealth."*

F1: Yes. You should read an article (forgot the name).

Closing Remarks

F1: What’s the meaning of your name?

Me: Harsh means "happiness" and Pra means "extra," so X means "extra happiness."

Verdict: Straight Convert!
